# 2026 Architecture Integration Plan

Sources: `references/2026 (1).pdf` and `references/case studies.pdf`.

## Objectives
- Align ATLAS with the 2026 Architecture (Maslowian Ascent Ladder: Regulation → Readiness → Renewal).
- Operationalize pressure navigation and collective memory across three levels: Participants, Public Defender Stations, County Commons.
- Preserve design constraints: no scoring of people, role-based visibility, navigation under pressure (“make the next safe move obvious”).

## Data Model Extensions (Firestore)
- Participants (`public/data/enrollees/{id}`):
  - `phenotypeId`, `journeyStrip` (phases, stops, milestones, currentProgress), `radialLoad` (zCodes + density/severity), `ipf` (current + history), `reciprocity` evidence, `allostaticLoad` trend (optional).
- Partner Stations (`partners/{partnerId}`):
  - `zBurdenSurvey`, `watershedWeights`, `stripMap` (survey → impact report → MOUs/pilots/tools/policies/eval → intervention), `pilotStatus`, `referralsManaged`, `civicYield` (org-level reciprocity).
- County Commons (`regions/{regionId}` or `countyCommons/{id}`):
  - `watershedWeightsAggregate`, `zBurdenAggregate`, `leadersEngaged`, `agendaIterations`, `incubators`, `actionTeams`, `quickWins`, `campaigns`, `impactReports`, `civicYield` (economic, policy, crime reduction), `stripMap` for regional maturation.

## UI/UX Surfaces
- Participant “Phenotype Journey” tab:
  - Strip map (Regulation → Readiness → Renewal), radial load wheel, IPF trend, renewal receipts (valued-role functioning, civic participation), milestone feed posts with attachments.
- Partner Station page (Public Defender):
  - Station strip map with gates/unlocks, org radial load from z-code survey, pilot tracker (eligibility/MOUs/workshops/tools/policies/eval), org civic yield outcomes.
- County Commons dashboard:
  - Regional strip map, watershed-weighted radial load, scorecards for leaders/agenda/incubators/action teams/quick wins (CPTED/prosocial pilots), campaigns, impact reports; trendlines for z-burden alleviation and civic yield.
- Navigation:
  - Add “Public Defender Station” (partner roles) and “County Commons” (admin/enrollment-manager) entries; concise CTAs that surface the next safe move under pressure.

## Logic & Analytics
- Compute z-code density/severity deltas, readiness from milestone completion, renewal from IPF improvement + non-reaccumulation of z-codes, reciprocity badges.
- Routing constraints incorporate partner capacity/pain-point surveys and watershed weights to propose the safest next move.
- Collective memory: persist strip-map events as timeline entries; feeds per milestone for participant, partner station, county.

## Security & Roles
- Partners: see their station + assigned referrals.
- Enrollment Managers/Admins: participant views; admin-only county view.
- Enforce “no scoring of people—only domain risk configurations”; keep existing permissions model and extend for new collections.

## Sample/Seed Data
- Add phenotype exemplars (SV, SP, RR) with strip maps, z-burdens, IPF placeholders.
- Seed one public defender station (z-burden survey, watershed-weight strip, pilot gates).
- Seed one county commons record (planning/quick-win/campaign milestones, mock watershed weights).
- Update `scripts/seed-*` to load new collections.

## Component Reuse
- Generalize the journey-strip component to accept data-driven configs for participant, partner station, and county views.
- Reuse radial load visualization with different weighting sources (participant z-codes, partner survey, regional aggregation).

## Execution Order
1) Schema/types: add Firestore types for `journeyStrip`, `radialLoad`, `ipfHistory`, `partnerStation`, `countyCommons`; update security rules.
2) Sample data & seed scripts: extend `sample-data/` and seeding for phenotypes, station, county.
3) Navigation/routes/pages: add Partner Station and County Commons views; wire to mock data first.
4) Components: data-driven strip-map + radial load widgets; milestone feeds.
5) Analytics: z-code deltas, readiness/renewal checks, reciprocity badges; safest-next-move prompts.
6) QA: role-gated visibility, sample data rendering, viewport checks, and basic lints.
